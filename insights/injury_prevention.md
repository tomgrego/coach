# Injury Prevention & Longevity

## The "MCL Protocol"
- **Status:** Rehab completed, currently in performance mode.
- **Rules:** 
  - Avoid high-impact landings (down-climb boulders).
  - Use 170-172 spm cadence for running to reduce knee shear.
  - Maintain glute and hip stability (Cossack Squats, Single-leg Bridges) to ensure proper knee tracking.
- **Caution:** Be mindful of "weird" lateral movements in climbing (extreme drop-knees or high-steps).

## Overtraining Signals
- **Resting HR:** A rise of 5+ bpm over the weekly average is a signal to deload.
- **The "Above the Neck" Rule:** If you have a sore throat or head cold, you can train lightly. If it moves to the chest (cough, fever), STOP immediately.
- **CNS Fatigue:** Feeling "electrically" tired, lack of "snap," or poor coordination. Often follows 190+ bpm efforts.

## Finger Health
- **Warm-up:** Always use light "flick" lifts or easy routes before hitting max recruitment.
- **Sanding:** Keep calluses thin and flat. Hotspots in the palm are a sign of friction-based over-gripping.
- **No-Hangs:** Safer than hangboarding for high-load recruitment when systemically tired.
