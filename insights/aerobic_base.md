# Aerobic Base & Recovery Science

## The "New Alpinism" Approach
- **Aerobic Deficiency Syndrome (ADS):** Characterized by a high heart rate spike during low-intensity movement.
- **The Cure:** Consistent, long duration in Zone 2 ($122\text{--}147\text{ bpm}$ based on $204\text{ bpm}$ max).
- **The "Floor" vs. "Ceiling":** Zone 2 builds the floor (durability/efficiency); VO2 Max intervals build the ceiling (raw power). You cannot hold a high ceiling without a solid floor.

## Recovery Metrics
- **Heart Rate Variability (HRV):** A high HRV indicates a recovered nervous system. A drop suggests high stress (training or life).
- **Active Recovery:** Light movement (Zone 1/2) that increases blood flow without adding "acid" or mechanical stress.
- **The "Stone" Clearance:** Long, slow runs help flush the metabolic waste from "Stone Forearm" sessions.

## 7a Climbing Benefits
- **Recovery on the Wall:** A high aerobic base allows you to recover $30\%$ faster at a mid-route shake-out.
- **Session Capacity:** Allows you to climb 12+ routes in a session without a terminal "flash pump."
- **Capillarization:** Building the "micro-pipes" in the forearms to deliver oxygen even under heavy tension.
