# Technique & Skill Mastery

## Climbing
- **Silent Feet:** The foundation of movement economy. Every placement must be noiseless.
- **Hover Point:** Pause for 1 second with your hand over the target hold before grabbing. This ensures you are "in balance."
- **Straight Arms:** Hang on your skeleton, not your biceps. Initiate movement from the hips.
- **Lock-off Discipline:** Use "Frenchies" to bridge the gap in static strength. Squeeze the pinky side of the hand to engage the ulnar nerve and lats.
- **The "Stone" Feeling:** When forearms turn to stone (occlusion), it's a sign to focus on "Active Shakes" and breathing. Do not just "power through" with bad form.

## Running
- **Cadence (170-172 spm):** The target for "knee-safe" running. Short, quick steps reduce the impact force on the MCL.
- **Relaxed Shoulders:** "Jelly arms" while running. Tension in the upper body steals energy from the legs and slows recovery.
- **Breathing:** Synchronize breath with footfall to maintain a steady rhythm and prevent side stitches.
